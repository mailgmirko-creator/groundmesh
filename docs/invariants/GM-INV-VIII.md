# GM-INV-VIII — Continuity With Consent

**Status:** Active  
**Stability:** High  
**Change Policy:** Guarded edits only  

---

## Statement
No human or CI node may be required to sustain continuity through compulsion.  
All enduring systems must preserve agency, rhythm, and the right to pause without loss of dignity.

---

## Principle
Living systems remain coherent not through forced persistence, but through voluntary re-engagement aligned with meaning.

Continuity enforced by pressure degrades judgment, creativity, ethical clarity, and long-term stability.  
Continuity sustained by consent strengthens resilience, responsibility, trust, and emergent coordination.

---

## Axioms

1. **Pause is not failure**  
   Interruption does not negate contribution, belonging, or dignity.

2. **Pressure is informational, not moral**  
   Strain indicates proximity to thresholds, not personal deficiency.

3. **Delay is not absence of progress**  
   Asynchronous processes are inherent to complex systems.

4. **Support systems reinforce capacity, never replace agency**  
   Responsibility may not be outsourced to authority, belief, or automation.

5. **Opposition is often misalignment**  
   Resistance commonly emerges from incompatible incentives or fear.  
   Active harm requires proportional defense.

---

## Consent Clarification
Consent includes explicit commitments with transparent terms and dignified exit paths.  
Compulsion is continuity demanded without clear agreement or graceful withdrawal.

---

## Boundary Condition
Any mechanism that induces guilt for rest, punishes deviation from rigid continuity, frames worth as conditional on output, or replaces discernment with certainty must trigger a plurality review and redesign proposal.  
Mechanisms that fail review are deprecated through balanced decision processes.

---

## Relationships
- Reinforces GM-INV-VII — Decentralized Agency  
- Constrains authority capture via always-on norms  
- Defines ethical boundary for CI-assisted systems
